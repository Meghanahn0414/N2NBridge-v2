Decision brief · database platform
Why we must move off MongoDB Atlas — and where to go
A platform decision for the citizen grievance app. States the compliance problem driving the move, weighs the options, and recommends a cost-effective, industry-standard database.
In one line
Citizen grievance data is sensitive personal data of Indian residents held by a government-facing system, so it must live on MeitY-empanelled, India-resident infrastructure — which managed MongoDB Atlas does not provide. Because the data is fresh (no migration cost yet), this is the cheapest moment to standardise on an empanelled platform.
The problem — why Atlas can't stay

Conclusion — the cost-effective, industry-standard choice
Recommended
PostgreSQL, managed on an empanelled Indian cloud
PostgreSQL is the industry-standard open-source database — the lowest total cost of ownership, the largest talent pool, and managed offerings on every empanelled provider (ESDS, CtrlS, Yotta) and via managed Postgres platforms. Its JSONB columns keep the flexible parts of a grievance record document-like, so you don't lose Mongo's flexibility where it matters. Because the data is fresh, there is no migration to pay for — you simply start here.

If the team is committed to the document model: self-hosted or managed MongoDB on the same empanelled cloud is the fallback — it satisfies the identical compliance bar with the least application rework, at the cost of higher ops effort (or a managed-DB add-on fee). Both routes clear the residency/empanelment requirement; the decision is cost & standardisation (Postgres) vs. document-model familiarity (Mongo).

Net effect
The move is no longer forced — Atlas (Mumbai) becomes viable again
If you are a private operator storing only name + mobile number, not deployed on or procured by a government body, the empanelment blocker falls away and the compliance case for leaving Atlas largely disappears. MongoDB Atlas pinned to the Mumbai region is a reasonable choice, and the decision reverts to cost and preference. Keep basic hygiene regardless: pin the India region, take clear consent, encrypt in transit and at rest, and store nothing you don't need.
The requirement returns the moment scope grows: storing grievance text, addresses, location, photos or government case IDs; integrating official government workflows; being procured by or hosted for a government body; or handling children's or other sensitive-category data. At that point the empanelment analysis at the top of this brief applies again — so design the data model to stay minimal if you want to keep Atlas.
